Parents and Carers Sessions

Would you like support with:

  • Understanding the differences between sex, gender and sexual orientation, and how they might interact.

  • Discovering the wide range of identities and language used by young trans and non binary people.

  • Learning about the rights trans and non binary people have in education, workplaces and healthcare.


Then Gendered Intelligence’s Parents and Carers session is for you


We provide half day (3 hour) trans awareness sessions enabling parents and carers to broaden their understanding of young trans people’s experiences. The session is based around interactive exercises, encouraging participants to get involved in thinking through what gender means to us all and how you can better support your child / young person. We will create a structured, but informal space for you to explore the topics and to ask questions.

At the end of the session you will have:

  • An appreciation of how sex, gender and sexual orientation interact
  • Improved understanding of trans identities, terms and language
  • Increased confidence in supporting your child / young person
  • Information about helpful resources

Who should attend?

Parents, guardians or carers supporting a child / young person who is trans, non-binary or questioning their gender.

Why should I attend?

The subject of trans people / gender identity may be new, or fairly new, to you and the session will help you to gain an understanding of the basics.

  • You can learn about ways to support young trans people.

  • It’s an opportunity to work and share thoughts with other parents / carers / guardians who may be having similar experiences.

  • A good understanding of gender helps us understand some of our own approaches to gendered expectations.

  • Understanding more about trans people and their rights will enable you to stand up for the child / young person in your life with confidence, as well as for trans communities in general.

  • Having the opportunity to ask questions in a non-judgmental, safer space can help address any concerns you may have.

  • The sessions take place in a small group setting, typically of no more than 14 people.

  • All our trainers have a wealth of grass-roots community knowledge and experience to call on.
