Educational Workshops and Assemblies

Our Workshops, Lectures and Assemblies in schools are based on some key messages. To learn more about these, click here

Workshops and Assemblies in Key Stage 1-2

Our educational workshops can be delivered in any primary school setting in England and Wales. They aim to engage learning around gender diversity, raise awareness of trans identities and what it means to include trans people in our schools.

We can tailor sessions to emphasise particular areas of the curriculum. As well as LGBT History Month events, PSHE class or drop down ‘enrichment’ or ‘diversity’ days, we also offer workshops to connect with other subjects such as Drama, English, Art, History, Biology and General Science.

In certain contexts, an assembly might be a more practical option. Assemblies are 15 - 20 minute presentations to a large cohort of students, sending broad messages that reflect the school ethos of equality, inclusion and respecting difference. They aim to engage learning around gender diversity, raise awareness of trans identities and increase understanding of what it means to include trans people in our schools.

Gendered Intelligence provides a non-judgmental service that supports all educational settings to develop and improve their trans inclusion and gender diversity good practices.

We always aim to work flexibly and to meet your needs in your particular setting. Please do contact us to discuss alternatives. All our services can be mixed and matched to suit your specific needs and circumstances, or bespoked as required.

Educational workshops and assemblies at Key Stages 1-2 are useful in one or more of the following circumstances:

  • The school’s ethos is committed to equality and inclusion, but would benefit from an increased understanding of gender diversity and what it means to include trans people
  • The school wishes to prepare students for the opportunities, responsibilities and experience of later life and living in a diverse world
  • The school would like to tackle and prevent Homophobic, Biphobic and (HBT) or gender-based prejudice and bullying

At the end of each session, students will show an increase in their ability to:

  • Recognise and value diversity
  • Respect difference and our responsibility to others
  • Assertively challenge prejudice and discrimination
  • Discuss relationships and feelings
  • Understand gender as diverse and the impact of gender stereotyping

Workshops

Duration: Standard session is 1hr
Cost: £165 for an hour (plus travel expenses)
Numbers: Up to 35 students

Assemblies

Duration: 15-20 minutes
Cost: £165 for an hour (minimum charge is one hour) + travel
Numbers: Up to 300 students

Lectures, Presentations and Educational Workshops in Further Education Colleges and Higher Education Institutes

Our Educational sessions can be carried out in any Further Education College or Higher Education Institute across England and Wales. They aim to engage learning around gender diversity and trans inclusion. We tailor lectures, presentations and workshops to particular courses, such as PGCE, sociology, psychology, journalism, nursing and applied theatre among others. In addition these educational sessions can contribute to ‘enrichment’ or ‘diversity’ events, LGBT History Month events and Student Union activities.

Lectures, Presentations and Educational Workshops in Further Education Colleges and Higher Education Institutes are useful in one or more of the following circumstances:

  • The institute’s ethos is committed to equality and inclusion, and would benefit from an increased understanding of gender diversity and what it means to include trans people
  • The institute wishes to prepare students for the opportunities, responsibilities and experience of later life and living in a diverse world

At the end of the session students will show an increase in their ability to: - Recognise and value diversity and prepare students for the world of work - Respect difference and our responsibility to others in particular within our future roles as professionals - Assertively challenge prejudice and discrimination - Understand gender as diverse and the impact of gender stereotyping

Duration: 1-3hrs
Cost: £165 for the first hour, £150 for subsequent hours (plus travel expenses)
Numbers: Up to 100 students
