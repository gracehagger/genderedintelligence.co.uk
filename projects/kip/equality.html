Research and Studies

There is a range of reports available online that consider the lived experiences of trans people. However, we think that more research and knowledge needs to be carried out to provide more up to date and accurate data that considers the everyday lives of trans individuals. It is noteable that more studies have been carried out in Scotland than in England, but in the absence of other data, it would be a reasonable starting point to assume that the experience south of the border is similar.

Engendered Penalties: Transgender and Transsexual People’s Experiences of Inequality and Discrimination Press for Change and Manchester Metropolitan University, 2007

Your Services, Your Say: LGB&T People’s Experiences of Public Services in Scotland Stonewall Scotland, March 2014

Trans Mental Health Study Scottish Transgender Alliance, in partnership with TransBareAll, TREC (the Trans Resource and Empowerment Centre), Traverse Research and Sheffield Hallam University, 2012

Youth Chances A survey of the experiences of LGBTQ young people in England and the commissioners and service providers responsible for the services they need The Metro Centre Limited, University of Greenwich, Ergo Consulting, January 2014

Out of Sight, Out of Mind Transgender People’s Experiences of Domestic Abuse Scottish Transgender Alliance, 2010

Trans and intersex people: Discrimination on the grounds of sex, gender identity and gender expression European Commission, June 2011
